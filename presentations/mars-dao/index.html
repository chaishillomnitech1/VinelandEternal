Proposal Lifecycle

07 / 22

Every governance decision follows a transparent, community-reviewed lifecycle from idea to execution.

① Draft

Any citizen submits proposal with title, rationale, cost estimate, and expected outcome. Requires 100 $MIRROR staked.

② Review

Relevant Guild reviews technical feasibility within 7 days. Council may fast-track urgent proposals.

③ Debate

7-day public comment period. Amendments can be submitted. Q&A sessions held in colony forums.

④ Vote

5-day on-chain vote. Simple majority for standard proposals; 66% supermajority for constitutional changes.

⑤ Execute

Smart contract auto-executes approved proposals. Funds released to designated wallet, tasks assigned.

⑥ Review

30-day post-implementation audit. Results published on-chain. Proposer accountability score updated.

Quorum

Minimum 15% of circulating tokens must vote for validity.

Veto

Council can veto with 5-of-7 vote; citizens can override with 75% supermajority.

Fast-Track

Emergency proposals bypass debate phase; execute in 48 hours.

Stake Return

Proposer's stake refunded if proposal passes or achieves 10%+ support.

Four Accountability Mechanisms

11 / 22

1. Recall Elections

Any Council member can be recalled mid-term if 20% of active token holders sign a recall petition on-chain. A special election is triggered within 30 days. Prevents entrenchment and ensures continuous democratic legitimacy.

2. On-Chain Audit Trail

Every vote, treasury transaction, communication record, and governance action by Council members is permanently recorded on Polygon. Any settler can inspect any record at any time. Immutable and tamper-proof.

3. Performance Scoring

Each Council member carries a public reputation NFT updated after every proposal outcome. Scores factor in: proposal success rate, voter turnout on their initiatives, emergency response times, and community satisfaction ratings.

4. Constitutional Review

An independent 5-member Review Panel (selected by lottery from registered settlers) audits Council actions quarterly. May refer violations to Justice Arbiter. Panel members rotate every 90 days — no political capture.

Governance Security Measures

15 / 22

Smart Contract Audits

All governance contracts undergo three independent audits before deployment: Certik, Trail of Bits, and an internal Mars DAO Security Guild review. Audit reports are published on-chain and IPFS.

Multi-Factor Identity

Settler identity uses a biometric + hardware wallet + settlement registry triple-factor system. Prevents Sybil attacks where one actor creates multiple fake voter identities.

Bug Bounty Program

Up to 500,000 $MIRROR rewarded for discovering critical governance vulnerabilities. Program runs continuously with a dedicated Security Guild managing submissions and triage.

Circuit Breakers

Automated on-chain circuit breakers pause governance if anomalous voting patterns are detected — e.g., a single wallet accumulating >30% of active voting power within 24 hours.

Anti-Manipulation Safeguards

16 / 22

Vote Buying Prevention

Votes are committed in an encrypted commit-reveal scheme. Vote direction is hidden until the voting period closes, making vote buying impossible — buyers cannot verify compliance.

Quadratic Voting Option

High-stakes constitutional votes use quadratic voting — each additional vote costs exponentially more tokens, neutralizing whale dominance and amplifying broad community consensus.

Time-Locked Stakes

Governance tokens must be staked 30 days before a vote to count. Prevents flash-loan attacks where an attacker borrows massive token supply solely to swing one vote.

Sybil Resistance

On-site biometric registry links each settler to exactly one governance identity. No wallet duplication. External Earth participants verified via ZK-proof identity attestations.

Concentration Limits

No single wallet may hold more than 5% of total supply without a special multi-sig Council approval. Automatic token split into trust structure if limit is exceeded.

AI Anomaly Detection

On-chain ML oracle monitors voting patterns in real time. Flags coordinated manipulation attempts for Security Guild review. Operates transparently — model weights published on IPFS.
